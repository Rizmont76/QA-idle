Vision

QA Idle is an incremental game about the evolution of a QA Engineer's career. The player begins as a Junior Manual QA, gradually climbs the career ladder, builds teams, manages entire organizations, and eventually expands far beyond realistic boundaries into increasingly absurd and entertaining scales of responsibility.

Vision Statement

QA Idle aims to become an incremental game where career progression feels tangible, not because numbers become larger, but because the player's role, responsibilities, mechanics and interface constantly evolve.

The game should create the feeling that every promotion is a meaningful milestone rather than a simple statistical upgrade.

The player should always be excited to discover what the next career stage unlocks.

Player Fantasy

The core fantasy is simple:

Start as a Junior QA. End as the greatest QA in existence.

The journey begins in a realistic software company, but gradually expands into larger and more ambitious scales.

Progression follows an intentionally escalating fantasy:

Junior QA
Middle QA
Senior QA
QA Lead
Head of QA
Director
CTO
Own QA Company
Multiple Companies
Planet-wide QA
Interplanetary QA
Beyond...

The transition should feel natural rather than abrupt, allowing the player to slowly accept increasingly absurd goals as the game progresses.

Core Experience Goals

Every major design decision should reinforce one or more of these emotions.

The player should feel:

Continuous career growth.
Excitement when discovering new gameplay layers.
Relaxed progression suitable for idle gameplay.
Satisfaction from building efficient systems.
Occasional humor inspired by software development culture.
"Wow" moments whenever a completely new mechanic or resource is unlocked.
Target Audience

QA Idle is primarily designed for:

Players who enjoy incremental and idle games.
QA Engineers and software testers.
Players with little or no knowledge of software testing who simply enjoy progression-based games.

The game should remain understandable even for players unfamiliar with QA terminology.

Game Identity

QA Idle is not intended to be another idle game where numbers simply grow forever.

Its identity is based on four core ideas:

Every major promotion unlocks new gameplay.
The interface evolves together with the player's career.
Career progression is the primary reward.
New mechanics are more exciting than larger numbers.

The player should frequently feel:

"I unlocked an entirely new way to play."

instead of:

"My income increased by 15%."

Design Pillars
1. Every Rank Changes the Game

Promotions should introduce entirely new gameplay systems whenever possible.

Higher ranks should feel like entering a new chapter rather than receiving a larger income multiplier.

2. UI Is Gameplay

The interface is part of progression.

New panels, windows, dashboards and management tools should gradually appear as the player's career advances.

The player should be able to see their growth simply by looking at the UI.

3. Progress Through Discovery

The strongest motivation comes from discovering something new.

This includes:

new mechanics;
new resources;
new currencies;
new progression systems;
new management layers.

Discovery should always be more valuable than raw numerical growth.

4. Easy to Learn, Rewarding to Optimize

The game should be approachable for casual players.

However, players who carefully optimize upgrades, economy and progression should advance noticeably faster.

Optimization should feel rewarding without becoming mandatory.

5. From Reality to Absurd Scale

The game starts grounded in a believable QA career.

As progression continues, responsibilities become increasingly exaggerated:

testing products;
managing teams;
managing companies;
leading global QA efforts;
eventually testing Mars and beyond.

The absurdity should emerge gradually so the player naturally accepts each new level of scale.

6. Humor Supports the Theme

Humor exists to reinforce the atmosphere.

Most jokes should reference software development culture, QA engineering, debugging and workplace experiences.

Comedy should enhance immersion rather than dominate it.

7. Learning Without Teaching

Whenever possible, real QA concepts should naturally appear as gameplay elements.

Players may encounter authentic terminology, testing techniques and industry practices through upgrades and progression.

The game should reward curiosity without feeling educational.

Long-Term Vision

QA Idle should feel like multiple incremental games connected into one continuous journey.

Instead of endlessly increasing production values, players continuously unlock entirely new gameplay layers that redefine how the game is played.

Each prestige cycle should introduce additional mechanics, professions or progression paths that encourage another playthrough.

Non-Goals

QA Idle is intentionally not designed around:

PvP.
Multiplayer.
Pay-to-win mechanics.
Loot boxes.
Energy systems.
Forced waiting timers.
Advertisements.
Monetization that interrupts gameplay.

The focus should remain entirely on satisfying progression.

Success Criteria

A new feature belongs in QA Idle only if it supports at least one of the following goals:

Creates meaningful progression.
Unlocks a new gameplay layer.
Makes the player feel their career has evolved.
Improves clarity or usability of the interface.
Encourages interesting economic decisions.
Creates excitement through discovery.
Fits the overall fantasy of becoming the ultimate QA Engineer.

If a feature only increases numbers without changing how the player experiences the game, it should be reconsidered.

One-Sentence Vision

QA Idle is an incremental game where players begin by finding software bugs and gradually build the greatest QA career imaginable through constantly evolving gameplay systems, interfaces and responsibilities.
